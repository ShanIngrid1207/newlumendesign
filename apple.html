Straight answers

The questions every contractor asks us.

I've run on referrals for years — do I even need a website?

Referrals are gold, but they're a ceiling — you only grow as fast as your customers talk. A site doesn't replace word of mouth; it catches the people who hear your name, search it, and need to see you're real before they call. Right now, those jobs are going to whoever shows up first on Google.

I already get enough work from word of mouth.

That's a great problem — until a slow season, a lost referral source, or a competitor who outranks you. A site lets you choose your jobs instead of taking whatever comes. It quietly fills the gaps so "enough work" doesn't turn into "scrambling" the month things go quiet.

Is it really worth the money?

One booked job usually covers most of a build, and a good site keeps booking for years. It's a one-time cost you own — not a monthly fee that vanishes if you stop paying. If it doesn't pencil out for your business, the free audit will tell you that honestly, no hard sell.
