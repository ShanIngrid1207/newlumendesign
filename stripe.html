Straight answers

The questions every contractor asks us.

No sales spin. Here's what we actually tell people who are on the fence.

Referrals are gold — keep them. But these days even a referral checks you online before they call. If there's nothing there, or the site looks dated, some of them quietly move on. A site doesn't replace word of mouth; it catches the people your reputation already earned you.

That's a great position to build from. The point isn't more work for the sake of it — it's better work: choosing higher-value jobs instead of taking whatever comes. When you show up first on Google, you get to be picky, and you're not exposed if a referral source dries up.

One booked job usually covers the whole site, and you own it outright — no monthly platform rent. We don't lock you into contracts because we'd rather earn the next build by getting this one to pay for itself. If a free audit shows we can't move the needle, we'll tell you.
